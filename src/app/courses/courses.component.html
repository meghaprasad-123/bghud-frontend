Advanced Cardiovascular Life Support, also known as ACLS, is a protocol for managing patients who encounter severe medical emergencies such as cardiac arrest. The ACLS course teaches healthcare professionals to diagnose and administer care for an adult victim of cardiac and respiratory arrest as well as any other cardio-pulmonary emergencies.
Basic Life Support, also known as BLS, focuses on the stabilization of a patient before and after an emergency. The BLS Course is designed to enable healthcare professionals to recognize several life-threatening emergencies, provide CPR, use an AED, and relieve choking in a safe, timely, and effective manner.
